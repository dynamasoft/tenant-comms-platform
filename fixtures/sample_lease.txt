RESIDENTIAL LEASE AGREEMENT (SAMPLE — FICTIONAL, FOR DEMO USE ONLY)

This Residential Lease Agreement ("Lease") is made between Palmetto Property Management LLC
("Landlord") and Jordan Rivera ("Tenant") for the residential premises located at
1427 Sabal Palm Drive, Unit 3B, Orlando, Orange County, Florida 32801 ("Premises").

SECTION 1. TERM.
The initial term of this Lease is twelve (12) months, beginning January 1, 2026 and ending
December 31, 2026. After the initial term, unless renewed in writing, the tenancy converts
to a month-to-month tenancy that either party may terminate by written notice as required by
Florida law.

SECTION 2. RENT.
Tenant shall pay rent of $1,850.00 per month, due in advance on the first (1st) day of each
month, without demand, at the Landlord's office or designated payment portal. Rent is
considered late if not received by the end of the third (3rd) day of the month.

SECTION 3. LATE FEES AND RETURNED PAYMENTS.
If rent is not received when due, Tenant shall pay a late charge of $75.00 plus $10.00 for
each additional day the rent remains unpaid, up to a maximum of $200.00 per month. Any late
charges are deemed Additional Rent under this Lease. A $40.00 fee applies to any returned or
dishonored payment.

SECTION 4. SECURITY DEPOSIT.
Tenant has paid a security deposit of $1,850.00. The deposit will be handled in accordance
with Section 83.49, Florida Statutes, including the notice requirements for any claim against
the deposit.

SECTION 5. UTILITIES.
Tenant is responsible for electricity, water, sewer, and trash service for the Premises.
Where the Landlord advances or is billed for any such utility on Tenant's behalf, the amount
charged to Tenant shall be deemed Additional Rent and may be collected in the same manner as
rent. Tenant shall not allow any utility to be disconnected during the tenancy.

SECTION 6. USE AND OCCUPANCY.
The Premises shall be occupied only by the Tenant and the following authorized occupants:
Jordan Rivera. No other person may reside at the Premises for more than fourteen (14)
consecutive days without the Landlord's prior written consent. Unauthorized occupants are a
material violation of this Lease.

SECTION 7. PETS.
No pets or animals of any kind are permitted on the Premises without the Landlord's prior
written consent and a separate pet addendum. An unauthorized pet is a violation of this
Lease that Tenant may be given an opportunity to cure.

SECTION 8. MAINTENANCE AND LANDLORD ACCESS.
Landlord may enter the Premises to inspect, make repairs, or supply services upon reasonable
notice to Tenant of at least twenty-four (24) hours, at a reasonable time, and Tenant shall
not unreasonably withhold consent. Landlord may enter without notice in the event of an
emergency. Tenant's unreasonable refusal of lawful access is a violation of this Lease.

SECTION 9. EARLY TERMINATION BY TENANT.
If Tenant elects to terminate this Lease early, Tenant may do so only as permitted by law and
this Lease. The parties have agreed to the early-termination provision described in the
attached Early Termination Addendum: Tenant may terminate early by giving at least sixty (60)
days' written notice and paying an early termination fee equal to two (2) months' rent, in
lieu of ongoing liability for rent. Tenant remains responsible for rent and charges accrued
before termination.

SECTION 10. DEFAULT AND REMEDIES.
If Tenant fails to pay rent or otherwise defaults, Landlord may pursue the remedies available
under Chapter 83, Part II, Florida Statutes, including serving statutory notices and, if
necessary, filing an action for possession in county court. Landlord shall not engage in any
self-help eviction, lockout, or utility shutoff.

SECTION 11. ABANDONMENT.
If Tenant is absent from the Premises without notice for a period equal to at least one half
of the rental period while rent is unpaid, the Premises may be treated as abandoned to the
extent permitted by Florida law. Disposition of any personal property left behind shall be
handled as required by applicable Florida law.

SECTION 12. GOVERNING LAW.
This Lease is governed by the laws of the State of Florida, including the Florida Residential
Landlord and Tenant Act, Chapter 83, Part II, Florida Statutes.

By signing below, the parties agree to the terms of this Lease.
